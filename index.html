Lucas's Top10 Melodies

Music has always been one of the biggest influences in my life. It's something that pushes me forward, fuels my creativity, and helps me make sense of emotions that words alone can't describe. From the heavy, textured sounds of alternative rock and shoegaze to the raw emotion of punk and the electronic depth of experimental music, each genre speaks to a different part of who I am. Every song has the power to set a mood, change a day, or open up a new perspective. I'm drawn to artists who take risks and create something that feels alive—those who aren't afraid to be unconventional, emotional, or loud. These ten artists have shaped the way I think about music and creativity. They've inspired me to look deeper into sound itself, to appreciate how rhythm and emotion can blend into something truly so unforgettable, so i decided to theme my top 10 list around some of my favorite artists. Im looking to pursue a college education in music for a long while to get a stable grasp on music theory and to play at a level above my own. I love music more than anything and im constantly going to see shows with friends and party hard, ive been to about 4 different shows this year from my chemical romance to Narrow Head. Theres really nothing better than going out to experience my favorite artists especially with friends, when it comes to every show ive seen its always been amazing. Pallavi's Top10 Metaphors

English literature is one of the richest traditions in the world, reflecting the changes in human thought, emotion, and society over time. Beginning with the spirit of the Renaissance, writers celebrated human life, knowledge, and imagination, moving away from purely religious themes to explore love, ambition, and nature. As time passed, literature became more reflective, expressing faith, inner conflict, and the search for truth, especially during times of political and social change. With the rise of reason and scientific thinking, writers began to focus on human behavior, moral values, and social mannerisms, giving significant importance to clarity, balance, and logic in their works. Later, literature turned toward emotion and imagination, highlighting the beauty of nature, personal freedom, and deep human feelings. The growth of the novel brought stories of real people, their struggles, and their moral choices. Gradually, writers began to examine social injustice everywhere, poverty, and class differences, making literature a mirror of life. Each period built on the last, showing how language and ideas evolved with society. From poetry and drama to novels and essays, English literature grew into a powerful form of art that teaches, inspires, and connects generations. It remains important today because it helps us understand our human nature, history, and the endless power of our own words.
